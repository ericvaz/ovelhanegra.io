Approach

One thesis, read four ways
Every product is the same move, made for a different reader.

Spatial understanding used to demand expertise, fieldwork, or years on the ground. A researcher needed a GIS stack. A migrant needed to arrive and find out. A visitor needed a local. An investor needed a hunch.

We remove the prerequisite. Every Ovelha Negra product performs one epistemic act — making place legible — for a different kind of reader. Different buyers, different interfaces, one idea underneath: that spatial cognition can be delivered, precisely and honestly, through software.

That is not a portfolio of unrelated apps. It is a single research program, shipped.

About

The black sheep

Ovelha Negra is the independent software house of Eric Vaz, Professor of Geography in Toronto and author of seven books. It is the applied arm of a research program — not a collection of side projects, but one idea about spatial cognition, built into tools people can use.

We build opinionated software: honest, unobtrusive, made to last. No fluff, no noise. Every product earns its place by making some corner of the world easier to understand.

EST. TORONTO & ALGARVE · SPATIAL SINCE DAY ONE

Ovelha negra — black sheep. In spatial statistics, the outlier is the observation that refuses to resemble its neighbours.

That refusal is not noise to be smoothed away. More often than not, it is the signal — the place where the interesting thing is happening. We named the company after it on purpose.
